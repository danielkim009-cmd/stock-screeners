Foundations of Markets & the Trend-Following Mindset Core
6 topics · Why markets trend and how trend-followers think
▾
📈
What is a financial market?
Price discovery, buyers & sellers, market participants
🌊
Why do trends exist?
Behavioral finance, momentum, institutional flow
🧭
Market structure & macro context
Bull/bear cycles, volatility regimes, rate environments — when trend-following works and why
🧠
Trading psychology — Part I
FOMO, revenge trading, process vs. outcome quality — before real money is on the line
📋
Rules-based vs. discretionary trading
Why systematic approaches reduce emotional decision-making
🗂️
Reading a price chart
OHLC bars, candlesticks, volume, timeframes
Instructor note: Introduce psychology and macro context here rather than saving them for the end — they're foundational filters for everything that follows. A student who understands why environments matter won't confuse a bad market with a bad strategy.

Asset Classes & Instruments Core
5 topics · What you can trade and how each market behaves
▾
🏢
Equities (stocks)
Common shares, sectors, market cap; how equities trend
🔮
Futures & commodities
Contract specs, roll, why Turtle traders used futures

Currency pairs, 24-hour market structure, carry
💎
ETFs & index products
Diversification, leveraged ETFs, sector rotation
🔗
Crypto & digital assets
High-volatility trending markets; unique risks
Instructor note: Anchor each asset class to its trend-following characteristics — not just what it is. Ask students: "Would a 20-day breakout strategy work on bonds the same way it works on commodities? Why or why not?"
03
Screening & Opportunity Identification Core
5 topics · How to find trades before they happen
▾
🔍
Technical indicators for screening
Moving averages, ATR, RSI — as filters, not signals
📊
Relative strength ranking
Identifying leaders vs. laggards within a universe
🏗️
Chart pattern recognition
Bases, consolidations, VCP (Volatility Contraction Pattern)
📰
Fundamental catalysts
Earnings, revenue growth — the SEPA approach to fundamentals
🛠️
Building a watchlist
Daily scanning routine, tools (Finviz, TradingView, TC2000)
Instructor note: Reinforce that screening is about narrowing the universe to high-probability setups — not predicting the future. Have students build and maintain a live watchlist throughout the course.
04
Entry, Exit, Profit-Taking & Stop-Loss Core
6 topics · The mechanics of getting in and out correctly
▾
🚪
Entry triggers
Breakouts, pullbacks, pivot points; confirming volume
🛑
Initial stop-loss placement
ATR-based stops, chart-based stops, fixed-percentage stops
📐
Position sizing at entry
Calculating shares based on stop distance and risk per trade
🔒
Trailing stops
Locking in gains as price advances; N-based (Turtle) trailing
💰
Partial profit-taking
Scaling out at targets vs. letting runners run the full trend
🚫
Full exits
Closing rules: channel breakdowns, system signals, earnings risk
Instructor note: The most common student mistake is inconsistency between planned and executed stops. Role-play the decision under simulated drawdown to make it visceral before it's real.
05
Risk Management & Portfolio Construction Core
6 topics · Surviving long enough to let the edge play out
▾
⚖️
Risk per trade (R)
Defining R, sizing every trade to a fixed fraction of capital
📉
Drawdown & recovery math
Why a 50% loss requires a 100% gain to recover; expectancy
🎲
Correlation & diversification
Avoiding concentrated bets; sector & asset class correlation
📦
Portfolio heat
Total risk across all open positions; max concurrent exposure
🔁
Adding to winners (pyramiding)
Unit-based scaling; Turtle System 2 unit methodology
🧯
Max loss rules & circuit breakers
Daily, weekly, monthly loss limits; when to stop trading
Instructor note: Make students do the drawdown math by hand. The moment they calculate that a 40% drawdown requires a 67% recovery gain is often the moment risk management clicks emotionally — not just intellectually.
06
Trend-Following Strategy Systems Core
3 systems · Complete rule sets for the Turtle, SEPA, and Donchian approaches
▾
🐢
Turtle System 2
55-day breakout entry, 20-day exit, N-based stops & sizing, unit pyramiding, correlation limits
🌱
SEPA (Minervini)
Trend template, Stage 2 analysis, VCP pattern, fundamental + technical confluence, tight stop discipline
📏
Donchian Channel System
N-day high/low channels for entry & exit, ATR sizing, how it relates to Turtle System 1
Instructor note: Present all three systems side by side after students have internalized Modules 1–5. They'll see that the systems differ mainly in their universe and holding period — not in their underlying logic. Assign a paper-trade project where each student picks one system and follows it for 4 weeks.
07
Advanced Topics Most Curricula Skip Recommended additions
4 topics · The details that separate students who succeed from students who quit
▾
🔬
Backtesting & system validation
Running historical tests, avoiding overfitting, walk-forward testing, not deceiving yourself with cherry-picked parameters
🧘
Trading psychology — Part II
Managing real-money loss, overconfidence after wins, following the system when it's losing, journaling practice
💸
Transaction costs, taxes & account rules
PDT rule, short-term vs. long-term capital gains, commissions & slippage, margin requirements — all of which eat trend-following returns
🌐
Macro environment & strategy fitness
Identifying when your system is likely to underperform; adapting position sizing (not rules) to regime; distinguishing bad luck from bad process
Instructor note: The PDT rule alone will shock most beginners — surface it early rather than at the end. For backtesting, the real lesson isn't mechanics: it's building the habit of asking "Am I fooling myself?" before committing real capital to a system.
💡
Sequencing note: Psychology and macro context are listed in Module 1 and woven throughout, rather than saved for Module 7. Students who haven't experienced real losses need these frameworks before they build habits around the mechanics — not after. Consider revisiting them briefly at the start of each subsequent module.
