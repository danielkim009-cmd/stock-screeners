Key Observations

✅ Consistent Positive Returns

  • Strategy delivered positive CAGR in all 11 windows — never a losing decade
  • CAGR ranged from +9.1% to +21.7%, avg +12.2%
  • Win rate was remarkably stable at 55–58% across all market regimes
  • Avg win (+5.4% to +7.0%) consistently exceeded avg loss (-4.7% to -5.3%)

🛡️ Superior Drawdown Protection

  • Strategy max DD: -25.9% to -31.8% across all windows
  • SPY max DD: -19.3% to -55.2% — far more variable
  • In the 2008 crisis windows, strategy limited DD to -31.8% vs SPY's -55.2%
  • The trailing stop reliably prevented catastrophic drawdowns

⚠️ Struggles in Pure Bull Markets

  • Windows starting 2009–2012 underperformed SPY significantly
  • The 2009→2019 window saw SPY +387% vs strategy +229% — a once-in-a-generation recovery
  • Active rotation out of winners via monthly rebalance cost performance in straight-line uptrends
  • SPY had unusually low DD (-19%) in this period — easy to hold through

★ Standout Window: 2016→2026

  • Best-ever result: CAGR +21.7% vs SPY +15.1%
  • $100K grew to $713,485 — 7.1× in 10 years

Interpretation & Caveats

How to read these results

The strategy is a momentum + breakout filter. It excels when markets are volatile with clear leadership rotations (2006–2008, 2016–2026) and underperforms when a single sustained bull trend rewards passive holding above all else (2009–2013 bull run).

The drawdown protection is the most consistent edge: in every window containing the 2008 crash, the strategy suffered roughly half the peak-to-trough loss of SPY. For a risk-adjusted perspective, the strategy is compelling across almost all windows even when raw returns lag.

Survivorship bias applies: the backtest uses the current S&P 500 composition. Stocks that were in the index but later delisted (bankruptcies, mergers) are not included, which may slightly inflate results for older windows. Walk-forward design (no look-ahead within each window) is used throughout.
