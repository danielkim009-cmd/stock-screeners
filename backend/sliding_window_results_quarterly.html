Key Observations

✅ Beat SPY in Every Single Window

  • Quarterly rebalance beat SPY in all 11 windows vs only 5/11 for monthly
  • Avg CAGR +16.6% vs SPY's +11.8% — a consistent +4.8% annual edge
  • Even the weakest window (2015→2025) still beat SPY: +13.0% vs +12.8%
  • Avg final value: $473,341 — 4.7× starting capital across all windows

📊 Why Quarterly Beats Monthly Here

  • Monthly rotation generates ~1,050 trades vs ~385 for quarterly — 3× more churn
  • Quarterly lets winners run a full quarter before being re-evaluated — captures more of each trend leg
  • Avg win +12.9% vs monthly's +5.9% — holds positions long enough for meaningful gains
  • Fewer forced exits means less re-entry friction and missed continuation moves

⚠️ One Exception: 2016→2026

  • Monthly (+21.7% CAGR, $713K) beat quarterly (+17.6%, $504K) by a significant margin
  • This window had an exceptional concentration in mega-cap tech (NVDA +2000%, META +600%)
  • Monthly rebalancing happened to rotate into these names more aggressively early on
  • One counter-example in 11 windows — not enough to change the overall conclusion

🛡️ Drawdown Comparable, Alpha Much Higher

  • Max DD nearly identical: quarterly -29.1% vs monthly -28.8%
  • But quarterly delivers +4.4% more CAGR for the same risk — better risk-adjusted return
  • Sharpe improved from 0.73 (monthly) to 0.93 (quarterly) on average
  • 3 windows crossed Sharpe ≥ 1.0: 2008→2018, 2009→2019, 2013→2023

Conclusion

Quarterly Rebalance is the Stronger Configuration for S&P 500

The data is unambiguous: quarterly rebalancing beat SPY in every single 10-year window tested, with an average CAGR of +16.6% vs SPY's +11.8%. Monthly rebalancing only beat SPY in 5 of 11 windows despite similar drawdown profiles.

The key driver is trade efficiency. Monthly rotation generates ~2.7× more trades, each with smaller average gains (+5.9% vs +12.9%). Quarterly rebalancing allows the strategy's best positions to compound through a full trend leg before being re-evaluated — which is particularly powerful with the RS_20 ranking, as relative strength leaders tend to maintain their edge for at least a quarter.

Recommended configuration update: Switch S&P 500 from Monthly to Quarterly rebalancing for more consistent long-term performance.
