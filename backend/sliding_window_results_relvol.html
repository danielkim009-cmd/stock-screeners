Key Observations

Consistent Alpha — 11 / 11 Windows

Rel Vol ranking beats SPY in every single 10-year window tested, with an average CAGR of +15.9% vs SPY's +11.8% — a consistent +4.1% annual edge. Even the weakest window (2015→2025, +12.9%) still edged out SPY (+12.8%).

RS_20 Edges Out Rel Vol

  • RS_20 wins 7 of 11 windows, Rel Vol wins 1, 2 are ties
  • RS_20 avg CAGR +16.6% vs Rel Vol +15.9% — a ~0.7% annual advantage
  • RS_20's edge is largest in early windows (2006–2009 starts): +1.1–2.4% CAGR
  • In the 2010→2020 window, Rel Vol surprisingly outperformed RS_20 by +1.2% CAGR

Why RS_20 Tends to Win

Relative Volume measures today's trading activity vs. the 30-day average — it captures excitement and breakout confirmation, but not the stock's sustained outperformance of the market. RS_20 (stock's 20-day return minus SPY's 20-day return) directly ranks by who is leading the market, meaning the portfolio tilts toward momentum leaders rather than just busy stocks. In bear-market recovery windows (2006, 2007, 2008 starts), the distinction is most meaningful.

Drawdown Slightly Higher with Rel Vol

  • Avg Max DD: Rel Vol -30.2% vs RS_20 -29.8% — negligible difference
  • Both strategies cap drawdown well below SPY's -37.5% average
  • Drawdown is driven primarily by the 25% trailing stop and position count, not the ranking method
  • The higher drawdown in 2011–2026 windows reflects the COVID crash captured in those periods

Conclusion

RS_20 Is the Better Ranking Method for S&P 500

Both Relative Volume and RS_20 ranking deliver strong, consistent outperformance vs SPY across all 11 windows tested. However, RS_20 wins 7 of 11 head-to-head matchups with a +0.7% average CAGR advantage. Over 10 years, that translates to roughly $32,600 more per $100K invested on average.

Relative Volume is not a bad ranking method — it still beats SPY in 11/11 windows — but it measures activity level, not relative performance vs the index. When all stocks in the pool already qualify as breakouts (6-month highs, above all EMAs), the tiebreaker that matters most is who is outperforming the market, which is exactly what RS_20 captures.

Recommendation remains: S&P 500 → PCT_TRAIL 25% · 9 positions · RS_20 · Quarterly rebalancing.
